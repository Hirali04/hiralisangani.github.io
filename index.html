About Me

I am a prospective PhD student in Computer Science with a focus on AI for Science. My research centers on developing machine learning methods that can understand and leverage the fundamental geometric and topological structures inherent in scientific data, with particular emphasis on biological and molecular systems.

I am interested in geometric representation learning for biological and molecular systems. My research aims to design learning algorithms whose representations respect intrinsic topology, symmetry, and causal structure, moving beyond traditional Euclidean assumptions.

I have experience with machine learning problems across computational biology, physiological time-series, scientific imaging, and clinical applications. My work has involved topology-aware latent representations for circadian biology, causal disentanglement of environmental confounders, physics-aware learning for microscopy data, and robustness analysis of medical imaging models under distribution shift.

Research Interests

My proposed work focuses on three integrated themes: enforcing geometric priors in latent variable models of cellular dynamics, creating unified representations for multimodal and spatial data, and developing compositional frameworks to deconstruct and predict the effects of perturbations.

  • Geometric Deep Learning: Incorporating topological and geometric constraints into neural architectures to respect the intrinsic structure of biological and molecular systems
  • Causal Representation Learning: Disentangling causal mechanisms from observational data to build robust models that generalize across environmental conditions and perturbations
  • Multimodal Scientific Modeling: Developing unified frameworks that integrate diverse data modalities while preserving domain-specific physical and biological constraints

Learning Topology-Aware Representations in Biological Systems | Bangalore, India

September 2024 – August 2025

  • Discovered that standard autoencoders mapped biologically adjacent timepoints (23.9h and 0.1h on 24h cycle) to opposite ends of latent space, revealing fundamental structural limitations in linear manifold assumptions for circular biological processes
  • Implemented Variational Deep Embedding (VaDE) framework using von Mises-Fisher priors to encode circular geometry directly in latent representations, preserving biological continuity in circadian rhythm modeling
  • Developed Fader Network with gradient reversal layers to disentangle environmental perturbations (light exposure) from intrinsic circadian signals, demonstrating that robustness requires explicit causal structure modeling
  • Collaborated with Prof. Shaon Chakrabarti on topology-aware machine learning for chronobiology, presenting findings at Unifying Theories in High-Dimensional Biophysics conference

Vilnius University & Lithuanian Research Centre for Agriculture and Forestry

Encoding Physical Invariance in Scientific Imaging | Vilnius, Lithuania

June 2025 – Present

  • Identified that diagnostic signals from collagen fiber orientation in Polychromatic Polarization Microscopy (PPM) for cancer diagnosis are inherently rotational, yet standard CNNs lack rotation-equivariance causing generalization failures
  • Designed SimCLR contrastive learning pipeline incorporating local orientation histograms to enforce angular consistency between augmented views, achieving explicit rotation equivariance in cancer tissue analysis
  • Recovered seven distinct, subtype-aligned collagen signatures without supervision, demonstrating that physical invariance must be fundamental to model design rather than optimization afterthoughts
  • Collaborated with Prof. Arvydas Laurinavičius on physics-informed computer vision for medical diagnostics, presenting research at LMT Student Research Forum
